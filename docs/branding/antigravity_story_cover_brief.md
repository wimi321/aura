# Aura Built-in Story Covers: Antigravity Handoff Brief

This handoff is only for the built-in cards that still read as strong placeholders rather than final premium illustration.
Existing stronger painted covers can stay as-is for now.

Reference sheet:
- `docs/branding/antigravity-handoff-targets.png`

## Goal

Replace the remaining procedural poster-style built-in covers with final art that feels like premium interactive-fiction key art.
The covers should read like story entrances, not app placeholders, UI diagrams, or generic character portraits.

Aura is not selling "chat with a character".
Aura is selling "tap into a scene already in progress".
Every cover should feel like the one charged second before the story starts moving.

## Deliverables

For each target card:
- final PNG
- size: `1600x2400`
- no text baked into image
- no logos
- no watermarks
- keep existing filename exactly

Target files:
- `assets/images/characters/shadow-warden.png`
- `assets/images/characters/oath-arbiter.png`
- `assets/images/characters/last-train-keeper.png`

## Global Direction

Think:
- prestige limited-series key art
- deluxe web-novel or interactive-fiction cover
- cinematic but restrained
- emotionally specific
- one scene, one tension point, one implied decision

Do:
- prioritize scene tension over face beauty
- let environment tell story
- include a strong story object, clue, weapon, key, note, ticket, lamp, grimoire, chain, etc.
- keep a human presence, but not always full frontal portrait
- use asymmetry and depth so it feels authored, not templated
- make the image feel like something is about to happen in the next three seconds

Do not:
- make it look like a dating-sim key visual
- make it look like a generic anime character sheet
- make it look like a mobile game splash screen
- make it look like abstract symbols floating on a background
- overuse centered full-body posing with nothing happening
- add typography inside the image
- add obvious AI hallucination props or decorative clutter

## Composition Rules

These covers sit inside a tall mobile poster card and also bleed into the chat page.
So composition safety matters.

Required:
- reserve the bottom `22%` for dark, readable title overlay space
- keep the core focal subject within the middle `70%` of canvas width
- avoid putting critical face detail on the extreme top edge
- do not place essential story objects where rounded card corners will cut them
- leave enough clean contrast for the app title and excerpt to sit on top

Suggested reading order:
1. environment or light source
2. character silhouette / body language
3. story clue object
4. implied danger or emotional hook

## Palette Guidance

Do not force a shared palette across all eight covers.
Antigravity should choose what best fits each scene.
The only rule is that the tone should feel premium, grounded, and non-synthetic.
Avoid obvious "AI purple cyan neon" defaults unless the specific scenario truly earns it.

## Rendering Guidance

Preferred:
- painterly realism or stylized cinematic illustration
- controlled detail, not noisy over-rendering
- expressive lighting
- believable materials
- readable silhouettes

Avoid:
- plastic skin
- generic beauty close-ups
- over-smoothed fantasy wallpaper rendering
- poster layouts where the background is doing nothing

## Suggested Master Prompt for Antigravity

Create premium vertical key art for Aura, a mobile interactive-fiction app focused on Tavern-style scene progression rather than generic character chat. The image must feel like a story entrance already in motion: cinematic, specific, emotionally charged, and built around one tense scene beat. Prioritize environment, body language, and a clue object over beauty posing. No text, no logos, no game UI, no placeholder symbolism, no generic anime splash-art feeling. Keep the bottom of the image readable for title overlay. Deliver as a clean 1600x2400 vertical PNG.

Then append the relevant card brief above.

## Final Note

Do not try to unify these eight covers by forcing one formula.
They should feel like they belong to one premium app, but still represent very different genres:
- manor intrigue
- sect tribunal
- liminal train horror
- cyberpunk pursuit
- school lockdown horror
- rainy high-school romance
- dark fantasy ruin
- living maze trial

Shared quality bar matters more than shared surface style.
